# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Corewise is for Mac users who want a calm, trustworthy explanation of what their computer is doing: battery state, storage pressure, live performance, startup load, thermal basics, and app issues. They may be technical enough to recognize processes and folders, but they do not want a scary cleaner app or destructive automation.

## Product Purpose

Corewise helps users understand whether their Mac is healthy, what is currently slowing it down, and what safe next step makes sense. Success means the user can quickly answer: "Is my Mac okay?", "What is consuming CPU, RAM, or storage?", and "What can I safely inspect next?"

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, premium.

The product should feel like a serious Apple-native utility: quiet confidence, clear diagnostics, restrained visuals, and no exaggerated system-fix claims.

## Anti-references

- Aggressive Mac cleaner apps with red warning language, fake urgency, automatic deletion, or "one-click fix" claims.
- Generic SaaS dashboards with decorative cards, marketing gradients, or oversized hero layouts.
- Dense system monitors that expose raw telemetry without explaining what matters.
- Dark hacker-terminal aesthetics that make ordinary Mac health feel threatening.

## Design Principles

- Make the first read immediate: status, score, live CPU/RAM, and primary concern must be visible without interpretation work.
- Explain before acting: every metric should include plain-language meaning and safe next steps.
- Prefer trustworthy restraint over visual noise: native controls, system materials, precise hierarchy, and semantic color only for state.
- Never make cleanup feel automatic: Corewise opens paths for review but does not delete or alter files in the MVP.
- Treat unavailable data honestly: if macOS does not expose a safe public signal, say so clearly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Do not rely on color alone for health states; pair color with labels and icons. Keep motion minimal and respect reduced-motion settings. Maintain readable layouts in narrow macOS windows and support light/dark appearance through system-adaptive colors.
